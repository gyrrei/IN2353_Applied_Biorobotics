Video Pitch & Feedback

Posted by Arne Sachtler on 25 June 2018, 6:06 PM

 

Snaking Around in Challenging Environments

What topic have you chosen?
Research and applications of locomotion and control strategies of snake-like robots.

Why do you consider it interesting?
The locomotion of a biological snake is one of the few that is capable to move around in various unstructured environments, such as underwater, in trees and mountainous surfaces. If we can mimic this is robots, it gives us access to different environments and a variety of applications.

What is the relevance to biorobotics?
It is the study of how to mimic the locomotion of the biological snake.

How are you planning to present it?
We would like to present research projects and possibly interviews with the researchers, as well as simulations and the theory behind the control and mechanics. One part would be theoretical, with animated slides or live writing on a whiteboard with a voice-over. In addition, there will be presented some examples of applications of these principles. The video material will be taken from the research projects and sources online.

 

The starting point for the research:

P. Liljebäck, K. Y. Pettersen, Ø. Stavdahl, and J. T. Gravdahl. 2012. A review on modelling, implementation, and control of snake robots. Robot. Auton. Syst. 60, 1 (January 2012), 29-40. DOI=http://dx.doi.org/10.1016/j.robot.2011.08.010
